Dish of the Week: Chaulanfan

Chaulafan is an Ecuadorian version of fried rice. There are many different types of chaulafan: shrimp, chicken, pork, mixed. In most major cities in Ecuador you will find chifas or Chinese restaurants, these are usually the best places to eat chaulafan; you can either eat at the restaurant or take it to go.

Ingredients

  • 5 lbs chicken breast, boneless
  • 3 cups rice
  • 6 eggs
  • 1 white onion, diced
  • 2 carrots, diced
  • 2 peppers (1 red & 1 green), diced
  • 1/2 cup peas
  • soy sauce (to taste)

Preparation

  1. To cook chicken: cut breast (as desired), heat oil in deep pan, place chicken in pan and cover, cook over medium heat until chicken is tender and fully cooked.
  2. To cook rice: add salt & oil to water (according to taste), rinse rice and place in pot with water, bring to a boil, reduce heat to low, cover and simmer for about 30 minutes.
  3. To cook vegetables: heat oil in frying pan, place all vegetables in pan, stir occasionally until cooked.
  4. To cook eggs: heat oil in frying pan, place whipped eggs in pan,scramble eggs in pan as they cook.
  5. To cook Chaulafan: heat oil in wok, mix all ingredients evenly in wok, occasionally mix ingredients while adding soy sauce.
  6. Serve and Enjoy.
